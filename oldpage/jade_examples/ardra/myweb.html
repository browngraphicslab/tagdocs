Portabella Paprikash

Sometimes you want mac and cheese, but when you want something a little more saucy and flavorful paprikash is the perfect answer to your woes. Tomato and mushroom based and flavored with Hungarian paprika, sour cream gives the sauce (and your rainy day) the perfect finishing touch.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Portabella Mushrooms
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 4 large Portobello mushroom caps, sliced ½-inch thick
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon sweet Hungarian paprika
  • 1/8-1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 teaspoons flour (I use white whole wheat)
  • 2 plum tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2/3 cup vegetable or chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup reduced fat sour cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley, divided use

Steps:

  1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat and add the onion. Sauté until tender, 5-6 minutes. Add the garlic and cook another minute until fragrant.
  2. Turn the heat up to medium high and add the mushroom slices. Season them with salt and pepper. Cook 2-3 minutes until they start to soften, stirring frequently.
  3. Add the paprika, cayenne and flour and stir to combine. Add the wine to the skillet and cook until it is reduced by half.
  4. Add the tomatoes, tomato paste and stock and simmer 6-8 minutes until the mushrooms are cooked and sauce is thickened. Season the sauce with salt and pepper.
  5. Turn the heat down to low and stir in the sour cream and 3 tablespoons parsley. Cook another minute until heated through but do not let the sauce come to a boil.
  6. Serve the paprikash on a bed of spaetzle and garnish with the remaining tablespoon of parsley.
